Top 10 Mountain Roads Every Biker Must Ride

Jake Morrison
March 28, 2026 5 min read 24 Comments

There's something magical about riding a motorcycle through mountain passes. The crisp air, the sharp curves, the stunning views—it's an experience that every rider should have at least once in their lifetime. After years of riding across continents, we've curated our definitive list of the top mountain roads that deserve a spot on your bucket list.

1. Stelvio Pass, Italy

With 48 hairpin turns climbing to 2,757 meters, the Stelvio Pass in the Italian Alps is often considered the greatest driving road in Europe. The eastern approach from Bormio is particularly spectacular, offering views that will leave you speechless at every switchback.

"The road up to Stelvio is not just a ride—it's a religious experience for motorcyclists. Every turn reveals a new breathtaking vista that makes you want to stop and stare forever." — RideOn rider, Marco B.

2. Transfăgărășan Highway, Romania

Jeremy Clarkson famously called it "the best road in the world," and for good reason. This 90km engineering marvel cuts through the Carpathian Mountains with tunnels, viaducts, and dizzying elevation changes. The summit at 2,042 meters offers panoramic views of Bâlea Lake and the surrounding peaks.

3. Leh-Manali Highway, India

One of the highest motorable roads in the world, the Leh-Manali Highway climbs through five mountain passes above 4,000 meters. The raw, untamed beauty of the Himalayas makes this a bucket-list ride for adventure motorcyclists worldwide. Be prepared for challenging conditions—river crossings, loose gravel, and unpredictable weather are part of the adventure.

Essential Tips for Mountain Riding

  • Always check weather conditions before starting your ride
  • Carry warm layers—mountain temperatures can drop rapidly
  • Maintain a slower pace on unfamiliar switchbacks
  • Keep your fuel tank at least half full at all times
  • Let someone know your planned route and expected arrival time

Whether you're a seasoned rider or just getting started with adventure touring, these mountain roads offer something for everyone. The key is to ride within your abilities, take breaks to soak in the views, and always prioritize safety.
